Chronology of Sugar Cane in Tolima

  • 1800s

    1800s: Sugar cane is introduced into the department of Tolima, especially in warm climate areas such as the San Juan Valley, Ortega and Chaparral.

    Late 1800s: The first sugar mills were built in the region, where panela and other sugarcane derivatives were produced for local consumption.

  • 1900s

    1920s - 1940s: Sugar cane begins to be cultivated more extensively in Tolima. The mills are modernized and panela production becomes an important economic activity for the livelihood of many families.

    1950s: The Tolima region stands out as one of the largest producers of panela in Colombia. The rural economy of the department depends largely on the production of sugar cane and its derivatives.

    1970s - 1980s: With the creation of associations and cooperatives, sugarcane production in Tolima becomes more stable. Panela begins to be sold in other regions of Colombia.

  • 2020

    2000s: Panela production in Tolima receives incentives for its national marketing and distribution. More sustainable techniques are promoted to improve performance and reduce environmental impact.

    2010s: Sugarcane continues to establish itself as one of Tolima's key agricultural products, and advanced technologies are implemented in sugar mills to optimize production.

    2020: Tolima continues to be a major producer of sugarcane in Colombia, and local communities keep alive the tradition of sugarcane products, such as panela, which remains a mainstay in the Colombian diet.
